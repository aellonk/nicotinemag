ARTIST PROFILE

Grace

Ahlbom

Artist

From the artist

Grace Ahlbom uses photography and sculpture to open conversations surrounding narrative, replication, manufacturing, and tracing and pointing. She combines elements of photography, sculpture, and mixed-media installations to highlight the specific practices of certain sub-cultures and her own positionality within them. In the following works, Ahlbom pairs photographs taken at original sites with subjects recreated and photographed in the studio. She observes memorials of past musicians and replicates the devoted fandom that Rock & Roll generated; creating renderings of something that might be installed in the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame.

Intrigued by society’s idols, Ahlbom focuses on how culture idolizes arbitrary aspects of masculinity. Ahlbom’s eye for detail is carried through until the end as she continues to experiment with unconventional frame finishes. In Shane she uses the same maple wood as the guitar’s neck for the photo’s frame. In other pieces, she uses bleach, graphite or Manic Panic as a frame finish, drawing parallels and complicating the hierarchy between the subject and the tools used to represent it. In the future, Ahlbom hopes to continue with more site specific works. Her most recent publications can be found at Dashwood Books in New York, New York.
