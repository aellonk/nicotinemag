ARTIST FEATURE

Paintings

by

Zoe Hawk

Zoe Hawk’s work deals with the complex experience of girlhood, exploring adolescent anxiety, feminine identity, and belonging, often visually referencing children’s storybook illustration. The depicted narratives are meant to be sweet and somewhat familiar to the viewer, yet upon closer inspection they take a mysterious or unsettling turn. Sometimes conveying innocence and curiosity, other times confronting violence and fear, her paintings investigate both the joys and trepidations of coming of age.

Hawk’s paintings are intended to be allegorical and come from a place of broad cultural and social examination, however much of it is based on her own personal experiences growing up as a girl. The costumes, colorful dresses, mournful funeral attire, and matching uniforms depicted in her work signify various modes of feminine identity, while backdrops of rigid institutional settings, suburban lawns, and wild landscapes set the stage for the girls’ interactions with one another.

The characters inhabit their own little worlds and act out their own dramas; societal pressure and adult authority are for the most part only implied, exercising influence from the periphery, existing visually within the scenes only as confining walls or fences. These vignettes capture moments of suspension somewhere between childhood and adulthood, between the sweet idealism of youth and the dark realities of womanhood, as the girls struggle to navigate their social and physical environments.

Zoe Hawk’s work has been exhibited and published both domestically and internationally. She has attended artist residencies in Ireland, Norway, Belgium, and Qatar, as well as the Women’s Studio Workshop in Rosendale, NY. Notable exhibitions include From Pangs to Pangolins, curated by Trenton Doyle Hancock (Shulamazit Nazarian, Los Angeles), SPRING/BREAK Art Show 2019 (Los Angeles), Contemporary Art Qatar (Kraftwerk, Berlin), and solo exhibition Dreaming As The Summers Die (Glass Rice, San Francisco). Zoe is currently based in Columbia, Missouri.
