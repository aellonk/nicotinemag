ARTIST FEATURE

Vessels

by

Debora Cheyenne Cruchon

Vessels is a series of 8 pieces about exploitation of black emotional trauma. Human figures were intentionally not portrayed, but instead are depicted sculptural vases, objects of the daily life, that acts as Pandora’s box of the emotional states of the artist, while outwardly expressing commemoration, remembrance, and hope. The vases, more stern, of earthy tones, are as much a representation of the body, the physical, but also indicate a presence in the past, an ancestry. The use of the pastel was more layered for these objects, adding dimensionality, a presence that gives them more weight and stability in the image. They are made to stay, are somewhat perennial. At the top lighter, brighter color come out, suggesting plants, flowers. Their fluidity and organic shapes suggest an ethereal body, escaping of its container in which they were kept. The association of the two, vase and flowers, are a metaphor for vulnerability and expression. How much can be kept in, bottled up, how much can be shown, revealed.

Debora Cheyenne Cruchon is a french multidisciplinary artist based in Los Angeles.

Vessels, oil pastel on paper, 2020.
